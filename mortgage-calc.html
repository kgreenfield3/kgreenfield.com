MORTGAGE CALC REDESIGN

Improving User Engagement

Overview

Improving engagement of an interactive calculator by 54%

  • My Role & Responsibilities: UX Researcher; led all research initiatives associated with the redesign including initial exploratory research and iterative testing of prototype

  • Project Timeline: 2 months

Background

In early 2018, SmartAsset undertook a redesign of their Mortgage Calculator with the aim of enhancing user engagement on the page. Despite being a highly visited page with numerous backlinks, the calculator was not effectively engaging users. This lack of engagement, despite employing a typically successful search engine optimization (SEO) strategy, indicated an underlying issue with user experience (UX). Consequently, my team was tasked with enhancing the overall UX of the calculator to increase user engagement.

Goal: Improve the UX of SmartAsset’s Mortgage Calculator in order to increase user engagement metrics and SERP results

Research Process

We utilized a two-pronged research approach to gain a comprehensive understanding of our user base and evaluate the usability of the current mortgage calculator. By combining these research methods, we would be able to gather valuable insights that would guide our efforts in optimizing the user experience and enhancing the engagement and SEO ranking of the calculator. Example questions and learnings can be found in the Appendix.

✨ Research Questions ✨

  1. Who are the key user groups that utilize this mortgage calculator?
  2. What are the primary usability issues with the current design?

Exploratory Research

  • Objectives: Understand our user base, identify areas for testing
  • Methods: Consumer surveys, data analytics, industry data
  • Deliverables: User personas

Usability Inspection

  • Objectives: Identify usability issues and product opportunities
  • Methods: Remote usability testing, competitive analysis
  • Deliverables: List of UX/UI issues

Key Takeaways

✨ Who are the key user groups that utilize this mortgage calculator?✨

Primary: The First-Time Homebuyer

The research uncovered that the primary user of the Mortgage Calculator was actually first-time homebuyers, early in the homebuying process, NOT experienced homeowners as we expected. This insight shifted how we approached not only the redesign, but also the creation and presentation of the content.


✨ What are the primary usability issues with the current design?✨

  • Users focused on the inputs in the middle and missed the inputs on the left hand side at first glance.
  • There was a misalignment between the audience and calculator inputs. First-time homebuyers who were still in the process of searching for a home only had information about their future down payment and not their potential mortgage amount.
  • Users found it tedious to scroll up and down to view the accompanying visuals and adjust inputs, especially on mobile.
  • The amount of information being presented all at once overwhelmed the users.

The Mortgage Calculator Redesign

Key Design Updates

Given our heavy-mobile traffic, we adopted a mobile-first approach to help prioritize information, content, and optimize interactions for smaller devices.

After many iterations, we landed on the following key features:

  • Incorporated a sticky output on mobile to allow to better support the "playing around" user behavior.
  • Restructured the calculator inputs to align better with the "first-time homebuyer" experience.
  • Utilized progressive disclosure by collapsing a number of unnecassary inputs and secondary information to prevent user fatigue.
  • Updated the primary visuals to save space and modernize the output.
  • Added a “How We Got this Answer” section to provide a basic explanation of how we do our calculations.

Outcome

Success! The redesigned Mortgage Calculator saw significant increases in our user engagement metrics and SERP results.

  • User Engagement:
    • Clicks per user increased 53.7% overall (78.7% on mobile)
    • Average session duration increased by 26.1% overall (31.4% on mobile)
  • SERP: Our SERP position increased from 19th to 4th (first page of Google)

Appendix

Learnings

  • Conducting thorough research is crucial, our insights challenged our assumptions about the primary users, leading us to completely reconsider our approach to the redesign.
  • Adopting a mobile-first approach is an effective way to prioritize and reduce unnecessary page elements. It encourages thoughtful decision-making and streamlines the design process.
